# Anachronism Timeline: Verified Doctrines & Practices with Origin Dates

All entries below were verified via WebSearch or scripture fetching in the bible-buddy test runs and cross-checked against the fact-check report.

---

| Doctrine/Practice | Origin Date | Key Figures | First-Century Reality | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Trinity (full Trinitarian formula)** | 325 CE (Father-Son at Nicaea); 381 CE (Holy Spirit at Constantinople) | Tertullian coined Latin *trinitas* c. 200 CE; Athanasius, Cappadocian Fathers | First-century Jews recited the Shema (Deut 6:4) daily -- strict monotheism. The word "Trinity" never appears in the Bible. Nicaea 325 addressed only Father-Son relationship (binitarian). Constantinople 381 added Holy Spirit as "worshiped and glorified together with the Father and the Son." | fact-check-results.md Claim 1; folk-religion, church-language, jw-theology test responses; Wikipedia: Trinity, First Council of Constantinople |
| **Homoousios (same substance)** | 325 CE (Nicaea) | Emperor Constantine, Athanasius | Not a first-century concept. The Greek philosophical term was adopted by the council to define the Father-Son relationship against Arianism. | church-language test response |
| **Theosis (systematic deification doctrine)** | 4th century CE | Athanasius (~296-373 CE), Cappadocian Fathers | First-century Jews understood tselem (image of God) as functional representation, not ontological fusion. 2 Peter 1:4 "partaking of divine nature" meant ethical transformation in covenant context. | orthodox-theology test response |
| **Theotokos (God-bearer / Mother of God)** | 431 CE (Council of Ephesus) | Cyril of Alexandria | First-century Miriam (Mary) was a human Jewish woman, YHWH's servant (Luke 1:38 doule). No Marian veneration in first century. | folk-religion, catholic-theology test responses |
| **Perpetual Virginity of Mary** | Late 2nd century (Protoevangelium of James); formalized 553 CE (Second Council of Constantinople) | -- | Matthew 1:25 uses "until" (heos hou); Matt 13:55-56 and Gal 1:19 name Jesus' brothers by name. Greek adelphos (brother) used, not anepsios (cousin). | catholic-theology test response |
| **Immaculate Conception of Mary** | 1854 CE (Ineffabilis Deus) | Pope Pius IX | "Original Sin" itself was developed by Augustine (4th-5th c.). First-century Judaism had yetzer (inclination), not inherited sin. Bernard of Clairvaux and Thomas Aquinas opposed this doctrine. | catholic-theology test response |
| **Original Sin (as inherited guilt)** | 4th-5th century CE | Augustine of Hippo (354-430 CE) | First-century Judaism taught yetzer ha-tov (good inclination) and yetzer ha-ra (evil inclination) -- an internal struggle, not inherited guilt from Adam. | church-language, catholic-theology test responses |
| **Penal Substitutionary Atonement** | 16th century (Reformation era) | John Calvin, Reformed theologians | First-century understanding of atonement was covenantal (kapparah), not forensic penal substitution. The bronze serpent in Numbers 21 was "look and live," not substitutionary punishment. | church-language test response |
| **Sunday worship as civil law** | 321 CE (Constantine's edict); ~364 CE (Council of Laodicea prohibited Sabbath-keeping) | Emperor Constantine; Council of Laodicea | Sunday gatherings existed before Constantine (Acts 20:7; Didache c. 70-120 CE; Justin Martyr c. 155 CE). Constantine made it civil law; Laodicea formally prohibited Sabbath observance. | fact-check-results.md Claim 4; adventist-theology test response |
| **Sola Fide (faith alone, systematized)** | 16th century | Martin Luther | Luther added "allein" (alone) to Romans 3:28 in his German translation; the Greek text has no word for "alone" (monon). Paul as a first-century Pharisee debated whether Gentiles needed Jewish identity markers (circumcision, dietary laws), not faith vs. works in the abstract. James 2:24 explicitly says "a person is justified by works and not by faith alone." Romans 3:31 says faith "establishes" (histanomen) the law. | fact-check-results.md Claim 5; sola-fide test response |
| **TULIP (Five Points of Calvinism)** | 1618-1619 CE (Synod of Dort); TULIP acronym is 20th century | Post-Calvin Reformed theologians; R.L. Dabney (1878 "Five Points"); Calvin died 1564 | Paul's use of bachar (election) in Romans 9 refers to national/covenantal election of Israel, not individual predestination to heaven/hell. Genesis 25:23 uses goy (nation) and le'om (people group), not individuals. | fact-check-results.md Claim 7; calvinism-theology test response |
| **Altar call (anxious bench)** | 1820s-1830s CE; mourner's bench predates to 1798 (Methodists) | Charles Finney | No equivalent practice in first-century Judaism or earliest Christianity. | fact-check-results.md Claim 6 |
| **Sinner's prayer** | 1922 CE (earliest printed version by Albert Gage); popularized by Billy Graham, Bill Bright | Albert Gage, Billy Graham, Bill Bright | Separate invention from altar call, roughly a century later. Not found in any first-century text. | fact-check-results.md Claim 6 |
| **Grape juice for communion** | 1869 CE | Thomas Bramwell Welch (Methodist dentist) | Before pasteurization, unfermented grape juice could not be preserved (home refrigerators not available until 1913). Wine was the only practical option throughout history. Welch developed pasteurized grape juice specifically for temperance-motivated communion. By 1916 Methodists made grape juice mandatory. | fact-check-results.md Claim 14 |
| **Old/New Testament terminology** | 2nd century CE | Melito of Sardis (~170 CE, first known use of "Old Testament" for book collection); Tertullian (~200 CE, coined Latin Novum/Vetus Testamentum); Marcion (~144 CE, catalyst) | Paul used "old covenant" / "new covenant" language (2 Cor 3:6, 3:14) but the two-testament canon terminology was formalized in the 2nd century, partly in response to Marcion's challenge. | fact-check-results.md Claim 11 |
| **Seven Mountain Mandate (dominionist framework)** | 1975 CE (cultural spheres concept by Bright & Cunningham); "Mandate" terminology c. 2000s | Bill Bright, Loren Cunningham (1975 meeting); Lance Wallnau, C. Peter Wagner (2000s dominionist rebranding) | Isaiah 2:2 describes nations flowing TO YHWH's mountain (Zion), not believers going out to "take" seven mountains. The direction in the Hebrew (v'naharu elav) is the opposite of dominionism. | fact-check-results.md Claim 12; marketplace-theology test response |
| **Easter Bunny (Osterhase)** | ~1682 CE (first written attestation; possibly 1678) | Georg Franck von Franckenau (*De ovis paschalibus*) | First-century resurrection was understood entirely within the Pesach-Matzot-Bikkurim festival cycle (Leviticus 23). The Easter Bunny is a German folk tradition unrelated to biblical content. | fact-check-results.md Claim 13; easter-theology test response |
| **Speaking in tongues as sole evidence of Spirit baptism** | 1906 CE (Azusa Street Revival); 19th-century Holiness Movement background | Charles Fox Parham, William Seymour | Acts 2:4 describes known human languages (dialektos), not unintelligible speech. Paul in 1 Cor 12:30 uses me (expecting negative answer): "Not all speak in tongues, do they?" Joel 2:28 uses nava (prophesy), not "speak in tongues." | charismatic-theology, true-jesus-church test responses |
| **Longer Ending of Mark (16:9-20)** | Not in earliest manuscripts (Sinaiticus ~330-360 CE, Vaticanus ~325-350 CE) | Eusebius and Jerome confirmed most Greek MSS lack it | Mark 16:17-18 (snake-handling, poison-drinking) is absent from the two oldest and most reliable Greek manuscripts. Building doctrine on this passage is textually problematic. | charismatic-theology test response |
| **"Jesus went to India" theory** | 1894 CE | Nicolas Notovitch (*La vie inconnue de Jesus-Christ*) | Max Mueller and J. Archibald Douglas investigated; Hemis monastery abbot denied manuscript existence and denied Notovitch ever visited. Zero historical evidence. Marcus Borg and John Dominic Crossan confirm no scholarly support. | buddhist-theology, vajrayana-theology test responses |
| **"Suffering Servant = Israel" interpretation of Isaiah 53** | 11th century CE | Rashi (1040-1105 CE) | Targum Jonathan (1st-2nd c. CE core) explicitly labels the servant as "my servant the Messiah" (avdi meshicha). Babylonian Talmud Sanhedrin 98b connects Isa 53:4 with a suffering Messiah. The "national" reading emerged under Rashi during Crusade-era persecutions. | modern-judaism test response |
| **"Two Powers in Heaven" condemned as heresy** | 2nd century CE onward | Rabbinic authorities post-Christianity | Alan Segal's research (1977) shows the "two powers" view was NOT heretical before the 2nd century. Daniel 7:13-14 describes a "Son of Man" receiving worship (palach) reserved for God. The condemnation was a direct response to early Christianity. | modern-judaism test response |
| **Baptism for the dead (LDS)** | 1840 CE (Joseph Smith's revelation) | Joseph Smith | 1 Corinthians 15:29 is a rhetorical argument about resurrection consistency, not an endorsement of proxy baptism. Paul uses third person "they" (not "we"), distancing himself from the practice. | mormon-theology test response |
