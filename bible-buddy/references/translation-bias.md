# Chinese Bible Translation Bias Reference

Instances where Chinese Bible translations introduce theological bias not present in the Hebrew/Greek originals. Sorted by biblical order.

## Old Testament (Tanakh)

| Scripture | Chinese Translation & Version | What the Chinese Says | What the Hebrew/Greek Original Actually Means | Why the Difference Matters Theologically |
|-----------|-------------------------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Genesis 1:2 | RCUV: 空虛**混沌** | "Empty and chaotic" — 混沌 carries Daoist connotations (Zhuangzi's hundun, Daodejing's "something formed before heaven and earth") | תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ (tohu vavohu) = "formless and void" — a state of not-yet-ordered, not a philosophical chaos entity | Chinese 混沌 invites false parallels with Daoist cosmogony. The Hebrew describes a pre-ordered state awaiting YHWH's speech-act, not a metaphysical chaos. Luzhenzhong's 沒有定形、混混沌沌 is closer to the original. |
| Genesis 2:7 | RCUV: 有**靈**的活人 | "A living person who has a spirit" — implies a dualistic soul/body anthropology | נֶפֶשׁ חַיָּה (nefesh chayyah) = "a living being/creature" — the whole person as an animated entity. Same term used for animals in Gen 1:20-21,24 | RCUV's 靈 imports Greek-Platonic soul/body dualism into Hebrew holistic anthropology. The human does not "have" a nefesh; the human IS a nefesh. CCV's 有生命的人 and Luzhenzhong's 有生命的活人 are more accurate. |
| Genesis 2:7 | RCUV: 生命之**氣** | "Qi of life" — 氣 evokes Daoist/TCM energy concepts | נִשְׁמַת חַיִּים (nishmat chayyim) = "breath of life" — YHWH's personal, intentional act of blowing breath | Chinese 氣 suggests impersonal cosmic energy. The Hebrew emphasizes YHWH's volitional, intimate act (וַיִּפַּח / va-yippach = "he blew"). Luzhenzhong's 生氣 is slightly better but still carries 氣 associations. |
| Exodus 20:12 | RCUV/CUV: **孝敬**父母 | "Show filial piety (xiao) to parents" — imports the entire Confucian xiao system (obedience, ancestor veneration, three-year mourning) | כַּבֵּד (kabbed) = "make heavy/weighty, honor" — give parents the same weight of honor due to YHWH. A covenant obligation, not a social ethic | Chinese 孝敬 imports Confucian filial piety, which extends to ancestor worship (祭祖). Torah explicitly forbids communication with the dead (Deut 18:10-12). kabbed is a covenant term; xiao is a social-order term. The frameworks are structurally incompatible. |
| Isaiah 7:14 | RCUV: **童女**懷孕生子 | "Virgin conceives and bears a son" — implies biological virginity | עַלְמָה (almah) = "young woman of marriageable age." Hebrew has a specific word for virgin: בְּתוּלָה (betulah), which Isaiah did NOT use | RCUV/CNVT 童女 reflects Christian theological tradition (via LXX παρθένος), not Hebrew semantics. Luzhenzhong's **少婦** is the most faithful Chinese translation. This bias perpetuates the entire "virgin birth prophecy" chain from LXX → Matthew → Church tradition → Chinese translation. |
| Isaiah 7:14 | CNVT (新譯本): **童女** | Same as RCUV — "virgin" | Same as above: almah = young woman | Same theological import as RCUV. |
| Daniel 8:14 | RCUV: 聖所就必**潔淨** | "The sanctuary will be cleansed" — borrows Levitical purification language | וְנִצְדַּק (v'nitzdak) from צדק (ts-d-q) = "be restored to rightness / be vindicated." NOT טהר (taher = cleanse) or כפר (kipper = atone) | RCUV imports Yom Kippur purification language that is absent from the Hebrew. The verb means "restored to its rightful state," not "cleansed." Luzhenzhong's 正宗化過來 is closer. This mistranslation enabled the Adventist "Investigative Judgment" doctrine. |

## New Testament

| Scripture | Chinese Translation & Version | What the Chinese Says | What the Hebrew/Greek Original Actually Means | Why the Difference Matters Theologically |
|-----------|-------------------------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| John 1:1 | RCUV/CUV: 太初有**道** | "In the beginning was the Dao" — 道 carries Daoist philosophical connotations (nameless, formless, impersonal cosmic principle) | λόγος (logos) backed by Hebrew דָּבָר (davar) = "word/speech" — YHWH's personal, volitional, creative speech-act | Chinese 道 invites false equivalence with Laozi's 道. The Hebrew davar is personal command, not impersonal cosmic law. The logos of John 1 is relational (πρὸς τὸν θεόν = "toward God"), willed, and historical. Daoist 道 is non-personal, non-historical, and 無為. |
| John 8:32 | Luzhenzhong: 真理就必使你們得**自主** | "The truth will make you self-governing" — 自主 (autonomy) shifts the meaning | ἐλευθερώσει (eleutherōsei) = "will set free" — freedom from slavery to sin (as defined in 8:34: δοῦλος τῆς ἁμαρτίας) | Luzhenzhong's 自主 introduces an autonomy concept absent from the Greek. Jesus defines "freedom" in 8:34 as liberation from sin-slavery, not political or personal autonomy. RCUV's 使你們自由 is better here. |
| 3 John 2 | CUV (舊和合本): 凡事**興盛** | "Prosper in all things" — 興盛 carries strong financial connotations | εὐοδοῦσθαι (euodousthai) = "go well on the road" → "things going smoothly." Standard epistolary greeting formula, not a theological promise | CUV's 興盛 enabled prosperity theology to claim God's will is financial wealth. The Greek is a conventional letter-opening wish (like "I hope this finds you well"). RCUV's 事事安寧, CCV's 事事順利, and Luzhenzhong's 諸事平安順遂 are all more accurate. |
| Romans 3:22 | RCUV: **因信**耶穌基督 | "Through faith IN Jesus Christ" — assumes objective genitive | πίστεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ (pisteōs Iēsou Christou) is grammatically ambiguous: could be objective genitive ("faith in Jesus") OR subjective genitive ("the faithfulness OF Jesus") | RCUV footnote acknowledges the ambiguity (「藉耶穌基督的信」), but the main text chooses the Reformation reading. If subjective genitive, Paul is saying "through Jesus's own faithfulness," which fundamentally changes soteriology from "your belief saves you" to "his covenant loyalty saves." |
| Romans 3:28 | All Chinese versions follow Luther: 人稱義是因着**信**，不在於律法的行為 | "A person is justified by faith, not by works of the law" — assumes erga nomou = all human moral effort | ἔργα νόμου (erga nomou) in 1st-century context (cf. 4QMMT: מעשי התורה) = specific Jewish ethnic boundary markers (circumcision, dietary laws, Sabbath), NOT "all good deeds." πίστις (pistis) = אֱמוּנָה (emunah) = covenant faithfulness/allegiance, NOT mere cognitive belief | Chinese translations perpetuate Luther's 16th-century framework. Paul's actual question was "Must Gentiles become Jews to enter the covenant?" not "Can humans earn salvation by being good?" The mistranslation enabled sola fide as a universal doctrine when Paul was addressing a specific 1st-century Jewish-Gentile question. |
| Galatians 6:16 | CCV (新漢語): 願平安憐憫臨到他們，**就是**臨到神的以色列 | "...that is, upon God's Israel" — translates καὶ (kai) as epexegetical "that is," equating "them" with "God's Israel" | καὶ (kai) most naturally means "and" (additive), not "that is" (epexegetical). RCUV's 和 and Luzhenzhong's 也加給 preserve the ambiguity correctly | CCV's 就是 is an interpretive choice favoring supersessionism (Church = Israel). In Paul's consistent usage across all his letters, Ἰσραήλ always refers to ethnic Israel. RCUV and Luzhenzhong correctly translate kai as "and," preserving two distinct groups. |
| Hebrews 8:13 | All versions: **新**的約 / 舊的 | "New covenant" vs. "old" — implies replacement | καινήν (kainen) from καινός (kainos) = "new in kind/quality" (BDAG: "previously unknown, remarkable") ≠ νέος (neos) = "new in time, recent." Whether kainos here implies "renewed covenant" or "replacement covenant" is debated — Jer 31:33 puts the same Torah on hearts (renewal), but Heb 8:13's own language ("obsolete, vanishing") leans toward supersession. The grammar alone doesn't settle this | Chinese 新/舊 creates a binary replacement framework. BDAG defines kainos as "new, previously unknown, remarkable" — whether this implies renewal or replacement in Heb 8:13 is debated. Jer 31:33 puts the same Torah on hearts (renewal side); Heb 8:13's "obsolete, vanishing" language leans toward supersession (replacement side). The Chinese binary obscures this unresolved tension. |
| Luke 1:28 | Latin Vulgate → some Chinese: 滿有恩典 / gratia plena | "Full of grace" — implies grace is Mary's inherent attribute (active voice) | κεχαριτωμένη (kecharitomene) = perfect passive participle of χαριτόω = "having been graced/favored" — Mary is the PASSIVE recipient of God's grace | The Vulgate's gratia plena shifted from passive to active voice, as if grace were Mary's intrinsic quality. Chinese RCUV's 蒙大恩的女子 actually preserves the passive better (蒙 = "received"). The bias originated in Latin, not Chinese, but influenced Catholic Chinese translations. |
| John 14:6 | Luzhenzhong: 我就是道路，就是**眞實** | "I am the way, the truth(=faithfulness)" — Luzhenzhong uniquely translates ἀλήθεια as 眞實 (genuineness/faithfulness) rather than 真理 (abstract truth) | ἀλήθεια (alētheia) corresponds to Hebrew אֱמֶת (emet) = "firmness, reliability, faithfulness" — a relational/covenantal quality, not an abstract philosophical truth | Most Chinese versions use 真理, which in modern Chinese carries abstract/philosophical connotations. Luzhenzhong's 眞實 is closer to the Hebrew emet (covenant faithfulness). This is a rare case where Luzhenzhong corrects a bias present in other versions. |
| Colossians 1:27 | RCUV/Luzhenzhong: 基督在你們**心裏** | "Christ in your hearts/inside you" — suggests individual indwelling | ἐν ὑμῖν (en hymin) can mean "in you" (individual) OR "among you" (communal). Context (1:27a "among the Gentiles") favors "among you" | Chinese 心裏 (inside hearts) pushes toward individual mystical indwelling, obscuring Paul's corporate/communal point: the mystery is that Messiah is now present AMONG the Gentile community (covenant expansion). CCV also translates 在你們裏面 without noting the communal option. |
| 1 Corinthians 15:29 | RCUV: 為死人**受洗** / Luzhenzhong: **替**死人受洗禮 | "Baptized FOR/ON BEHALF OF the dead" — implies proxy baptism | ὑπέρ (hyper) has a wide semantic range: "for the sake of," "because of," "concerning," "on behalf of." RCUV/Luzhenzhong choose "on behalf of" (proxy), but "because of" is equally valid | CCV's 為死人的**緣故**受洗 ("baptized for the SAKE of the dead") better preserves hyper's ambiguity. RCUV/Luzhenzhong's proxy-baptism reading enabled LDS (Mormon) doctrine of baptism for the dead, when Paul may have meant "baptized because of the witness of dead believers." |

## Systemic Translation Patterns

| Pattern | Affected Verses | Nature of Bias | Impact |
|---------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| 道 for λόγος/דָּבָר | John 1:1-14; all "word of God" passages | Imports Daoist cosmological framework into Hebrew speech-act theology | Creates false Dao-Logos equivalence across the entire Gospel of John |
| 靈/魂 for נֶפֶשׁ | Gen 2:7; Ezek 18:4; throughout OT | Imports Greek-Platonic soul/body dualism into Hebrew holistic anthropology | Undermines biblical nefesh (whole-person) anthropology; enables "immortal soul" doctrine foreign to Hebrew thought |
| 氣 for רוּחַ/נִשְׁמָה | Gen 1:2; Gen 2:7; Ps 104:29-30 | Imports Daoist/TCM qi energy concept into YHWH's personal ruach | Obscures that ruach is YHWH's personal, volitional action, not impersonal cosmic energy |
| 義 for צְדָקָה/מִשְׁפָּט/δικαιοσύνη | Rom 3:21-28; Mic 6:8; Gen 15:6 | Single Chinese character 義 conflates covenant-righteousness (tsedaqah), social justice (mishpat), and Chinese yi (義氣, moral loyalty) | Enables false equivalence between biblical covenant-righteousness and Confucian/folk-religion concepts of righteousness (e.g., Guan Gong's 義) |
| 孝敬 for כַּבֵּד | Exod 20:12; Deut 5:16 | Imports Confucian xiao (filial piety) system including ancestor veneration | Torah kabbed is covenant obligation; Confucian xiao extends to ancestor worship, which Torah explicitly forbids |
| 混沌 for תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ | Gen 1:2 | Imports Daoist 混沌 cosmological associations | Creates false parallel with Daoist/Zhuangzi hundun, obscuring the Hebrew "not-yet-ordered" state |
| 成全 for πληρῶσαι | Matt 5:17 | Chinese 成全 can be read as "complete and finish" (implying Torah is done) | In rabbinic context, πληρῶσαι = קיים (qiyyem) = "establish/correctly interpret." 成全 risks being read as "Torah is completed, so we don't need it anymore" — the exact opposite of what Yeshua said in 5:18-19. |
