# Church Practices Assumed to Be Biblical

Many users assume certain church practices come from the Bible. This reference provides pre-verified first-century analysis for each practice.

**How to use:** When a user asks about any practice below, fetch the listed key evidence passages, then guide them through the first-century reality vs. what they assume.

**How to guide the user through these:**
1. **Fetch the actual text** — run the scripts to get Hebrew/Greek + multiple Chinese translations
2. **Show what the text actually says** — let the user read it, not your summary
3. **Show the historical timeline** — when did this practice actually start? Cite specific dates, councils, people
4. **Show what Yeshua and the apostles actually did** — their recorded practice is the strongest evidence
5. **Never say "your church is wrong"** — instead, present the evidence and say: "This is what the first-century text and history show. How you apply this is your decision."

## 1. Trinity (三位一體)

**User assumes:** Biblical teaching

**First-century reality:** Progressively defined: Nicaea (325 CE, Father-Son) and Constantinople (381 CE, Holy Spirit). Tertullian coined "trinitas" ~200 CE. The word "Trinity" never appears in the Bible. Yeshua affirmed the Shema (Mark 12:29 quoting Deut 6:4): "YHWH our God, YHWH is one (echad/אֶחָד)." Echad is the standard cardinal number "one" — the "compound unity" argument is not supported by standard Hebrew lexicography (BDB, HALOT). John 10:30 uses neuter hen (ἕν), not masculine heis — ruling out personal identity, though scholars debate whether it indicates shared purpose or shared nature. The Spirit (ruach/רוּחַ) in the Tanakh is God's active presence, not a separate "person."

**Key evidence:** Fetch Deuteronomy 6:4 (Hebrew), Mark 12:29, John 10:30 (Greek), Isaiah 43:10-11.

## 2. Sunday worship (主日崇拜)

**User assumes:** Biblical command

**First-century reality:** No biblical text commands replacing Shabbat. Yeshua/apostles kept Shabbat (Luke 4:16, Acts 13:14, 17:2, 18:4). Sunday gatherings existed early (Didache ~100 CE, Justin Martyr ~155 CE) but as additional meetings, not Shabbat replacements. Sunday rest was first mandated as civil law by Constantine (321 CE); Council of Laodicea (~364 CE) formally prohibited Sabbath-keeping.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Exodus 20:8-11, Acts 20:7 (Saturday evening gathering, not Sunday morning).

## 3. New replaces Old (新約取代舊約)

**User assumes:** Biblical teaching

**First-century reality:** Hebrews 8:13 uses καινός (kainos), not νέος (neos). Standard lexicons (BDAG): kainos = "new in kind, novel" (qualitative); neos = "new in time, recent" (temporal). Context determines whether kainos implies renewal or replacement — the grammar alone doesn't settle this debate. Jeremiah 31:31-33's "new covenant" (berit chadashah/בְּרִית חֲדָשָׁה) puts Torah IN the heart — it doesn't abolish Torah. Yeshua said "not one jot or tittle" will pass (Matt 5:18). Paul said "we establish the law" (Rom 3:31). The "old/new testament" terminology was formalized in the 2nd century, partly in response to Marcion's challenge; Paul already used "old/new covenant" language (2 Cor 3:6, 14). First-century believers read one continuous scripture.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Jeremiah 31:31-34 (Hebrew), Hebrews 8:8-13 (Greek kainos), Matthew 5:17-19, Romans 3:31.

## 4. Calvinism TULIP (加爾文主義)

**User assumes:** Biblical doctrine

**First-century reality:** The five doctrinal points were formulated at Dort (1618-1619) in response to Arminianism; the TULIP acronym is a later English mnemonic (~20th c.). Calvin (d. 1564) never articulated these exact five points. Romans 9's "Jacob I loved, Esau I hated" (Malachi 1:2-3) is about two NATIONS, not individual eternal destiny — read the Hebrew: goy (גוי) and le'om (לְאֹם). Paul's argument in Romans 9-11 is about God's faithfulness to Israel, ending with "all Israel will be saved" (11:26) — the opposite of limited atonement. Pharisaic Judaism held divine sovereignty AND human free will in tension (Josephus, Antiquities 18.1.3; Mishnah Avot 3:15 "all is foreseen, yet freedom of choice is given"). Proorizo (προορίζω) = pre-setting the destination/plan, not pre-selecting individuals for damnation.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Romans 9:1-5 + 11:25-32 (show FULL argument), Malachi 1:1-5 (Hebrew), Deuteronomy 7:6-11.

## 5. Sola fide (因信稱義)

**User assumes:** Paul's teaching

**First-century reality:** Systematized as formal doctrine by Luther (16th c.); faith-justification ideas existed earlier but not in this form. Luther added "allein" (alone) to Rom 3:28 — the Greek has no "alone" (monon), and James 2:24 explicitly denies it. Paul's πίστις = אמונה (emunah, covenant faithfulness), not mere "mental belief." ἔργα νόμου = halakhic boundary markers (4QMMT), not "good works." Paul affirmed Torah (Rom 3:31, 7:12).

**Key evidence:** Fetch Romans 3:28-31, James 2:24, Habakkuk 2:4 (Hebrew).

## 6. Altar call / sinner's prayer (呼召禱告/決志禱告)

**User assumes:** Apostolic practice

**First-century reality:** Altar call: popularized by Charles Finney's "anxious bench" (~1820s-30s); mourner's bench predates him (Methodists ~1798). Sinner's prayer: a separate, even later invention (~1922, Albert Gage). First-century response was mikveh (immersion) + Torah instruction.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Acts 2:38-42.

## 7. Christmas Dec 25 (聖誕節)

**User assumes:** Jesus' birthday

**First-century reality:** No biblical date. Dec 25 adopted from Roman Sol Invictus festival (~4th c.). Yeshua likely born during Sukkot (Feast of Tabernacles) based on Luke 1-2 timeline.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Luke 2:8 (shepherds in fields = not winter).

## 8. Tithe to church (十一奉獻)

**User assumes:** Biblical command

**First-century reality:** Levitical tithe supported the Temple system, Levites, and the poor — not a pastoral salary fund. Three separate tithes totaling ~23%. Modern "10% to church" is a simplification.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Malachi 3:10 in full context (3:6-12, addressed to post-exilic Israel).

---

## 9. Seven Mountains / 職場轉化

**User assumes:** Biblical mandate

**First-century reality:** Cultural-spheres concept originated 1975 (Bright & Cunningham); the dominionist "Seven Mountain Mandate" was rebranded ~2000s by Lance Wallnau and NAR. Isaiah 2:2's "mountain" is Zion alone, with nations flowing TO it — opposite of "Christians going out to conquer." Genesis 1:28 radah/kavash applies to animals/land, never human institutions. Yeshua's movement was anti-imperial (Mark 10:42-45 "not so among you").

**Key evidence:** Fetch Isaiah 2:2-4 (Hebrew), Genesis 1:28, Genesis 2:15 (avad/shamar = serve/guard).

## 10. Prosperity gospel (成功神學)

**User assumes:** Biblical promise

**First-century reality:** Malachi 3:10 is a national covenant promise to post-exilic Israel, not individual financial guarantee. 3 John 2 is a standard Greco-Roman letter greeting, not theology. Yeshua taught treasure in heaven (Matt 6:19-21), warned against wealth (Mark 10:23-25).

**Key evidence:** Fetch Malachi 3:6-12 in full, 3 John 1:2 with genre analysis.

## 11. Easter (復活節)

**User assumes:** Celebrates resurrection

**First-century reality:** The name "Easter" is English/Germanic only; most languages use Pascha (from Pesach). First-century believers celebrated resurrection within Pesach (Passover) framework. Nicaea (325 CE) deliberately separated the date from the Jewish calendar. Rabbits and eggs are medieval European folk additions with zero biblical basis.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Exodus 12:1-14 (Pesach), Leviticus 23:9-14 (firstfruits/bikkurim), 1 Corinthians 5:7-8 ("Christ our Pesach").

## 12. Devotional individualization (靈修式個人化)

**User assumes:** Personal promises

**First-century reality:** Devotional books like 荒漠甘泉 (Streams in the Desert) routinely strip national/historical promises from their context and apply them to individual spiritual life. Isaiah 43:2 = Israel's return from Babylon, not personal trials. Jeremiah 29:11 = promise to exiled Judah as a nation (read 29:10 first: "after 70 years"). Psalm 46:10 harpu (הַרְפּוּ, from root raphah = cease/let go) in the military context of Psalm 46 means "stop fighting/cease striving," not "be still in quiet meditation."

**Key evidence:** Fetch the FULL context: Isaiah 43:1-7, Jeremiah 29:1-14 (the whole letter), Psalm 46:1-11 (war context). Show the Hebrew verbs.

## 13. Spiritual covering (屬靈遮蓋)

**User assumes:** Biblical authority structure

**First-century reality:** Product of the Shepherding Movement (1970s, Mumford/Prince/Basham); adopted by NAR. No first-century concept of layered spiritual protection through human hierarchy. Romans 13:1 (exousia) addresses civic authority under Roman occupation, not pastoral hierarchy. Hebrews 13:17 peithesthe = "be persuaded by" (middle voice), not "obey without question." First-century synagogues had elders (zekenim/זְקֵנִים) as communal advisors, not hierarchical authority chains. The Hebrew kafar (כָּפַר, covering) is atonement vocabulary (Lev 16), not authority vocabulary.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Romans 13:1-7 (Greek exousia), Hebrews 13:17 (Greek peithesthe), Matthew 20:25-28 ("not so among you"), 1 Peter 5:1-3 (elders as fellow-elders, not lords).

## 14. Declaration prayer (宣告禱告)

**User assumes:** Biblical prayer model

**First-century reality:** "Declaring" or "decreeing" things into existence is Word of Faith theology (E.W. Kenyon → Kenneth Hagin, 1960s+). Proverbs 18:21 "death and life in the tongue" is wisdom literature about consequences of speech, not metaphysical creative power. Mark 11:23 "say to this mountain" is hyperbolic mashal (משל) about trust in God, not a technique for verbal creation. First-century Jewish prayer (see the Amidah/Shemoneh Esreh) was petition and praise to God, not declarations commanding reality. Only YHWH speaks things into existence (Gen 1:3 vayomer/וַיֹּאמֶר).

**Key evidence:** Fetch Proverbs 18:21, Mark 11:22-25 (full context), Genesis 1:3 (Hebrew), Matthew 6:9-13 (Lord's Prayer as model).

## 15. Binding and loosing (捆綁與釋放)

**User assumes:** Spiritual warfare technique

**First-century reality:** In first-century rabbinic usage, asar (אָסַר, bind) and hitir (הִתִּיר, loose) are halakhic terms meaning "prohibit" and "permit." When Yeshua tells Peter "whatever you bind/loose on earth" (Matt 16:19, 18:18), he is granting halakhic authority — the authority to make rulings on Torah practice, not to bind demons or claim territorial control. The Mishnah uses this terminology extensively (e.g., Mishnah Terumot 5:4). The "spiritual warfare" reinterpretation comes from medieval demonology → 20th-century charismatic theology (esp. C. Peter Wagner's "Strategic-Level Spiritual Warfare," 1990s).

**Key evidence:** Fetch Matthew 16:19 (Greek deo/luo), Matthew 18:18, compare with Mishnah usage. Show rabbinic parallels.

## 16. Anointing as transferable substance (恩膏/膏抹)

**User assumes:** Spiritual power transfer

**First-century reality:** Hebrew mashach (מָשַׁח) = to smear/anoint with oil, a physical act of commissioning for a role (king, priest, prophet). 1 John 2:20 chrisma = the teaching/knowledge from God, not a transferable spiritual substance. Luke 4:18 quotes Isaiah 61:1 where "anointing" = commissioning by YHWH's Spirit for a specific mission. The concept of anointing as a portable, transferable "power" that can "fall on" people or be passed through laying on of hands as a substance is a modern charismatic innovation (Latter Rain Movement, 1948+). First-century semikhah (סְמִיכָה, laying on of hands) was authorization/commissioning, not power transfer.

**Key evidence:** Fetch 1 John 2:20-27 (Greek chrisma), Luke 4:18-19, Isaiah 61:1 (Hebrew mashach), 1 Samuel 16:13 (David's anointing = commissioning).

## 17. Territorial spirits (屬靈爭戰/地域靈)

**User assumes:** Biblical worldview

**First-century reality:** Systematic "territorial spirits" theology was developed by C. Peter Wagner (1990s "Strategic-Level Spiritual Warfare") and George Otis Jr., not found in first-century Judaism. Ephesians 6:12 archai/exousiai refers to structural powers (political/spiritual forces in Paul's Roman context), not geographically assigned demons. Daniel 10:13 "prince of Persia" (sar/שַׂר) reflects Second Temple angelology (each nation has a heavenly patron), but this is descriptive narrative, not a model for "spiritual mapping" or "prayer walking." First-century Jews did practice exorcism (Josephus, Ant. 8.2.5), but as individual healing, not strategic territorial warfare.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Ephesians 6:10-18 (Greek archai, exousiai, kosmokratoras), Daniel 10:13-14 (Hebrew sar), Mark 5:1-13 (individual exorcism).

## 18. Five-fold ministry as permanent offices (五重職分)

**User assumes:** Biblical church governance

**First-century reality:** Ephesians 4:11 lists apostolous, prophetas, euangelistas, poimenas kai didaskalous as gifts (domata) Christ gave to the church, not as a permanent governance hierarchy. The NAR interpretation of these as five ongoing authoritative offices (esp. "apostles" and "prophets" with binding authority) was developed by C. Peter Wagner (~1990s-2000s). In first-century context, apostolos (ἀπόστολος) = shaliach (שָׁלִיחַ), a commissioned agent for a specific task (not a permanent rank). Paul called himself apostolos but also called Epaphroditus one (Phil 2:25). Prophets in first-century Judaism were tested by outcomes (Deut 18:22), not elevated to unchallengeable authority.

**Key evidence:** Fetch Ephesians 4:11-16 (Greek domata), 1 Corinthians 12:28-30 (gifts as body metaphor, not hierarchy), Deuteronomy 18:20-22 (prophet testing).
