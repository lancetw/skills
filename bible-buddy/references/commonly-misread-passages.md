# Commonly Misread Scripture Passages

Extracted from 37 with-skill test responses (iteration-3). Each passage was identified as "commonly misread" or "taken out of context" in at least one response. Sorted by biblical order (Genesis to Revelation).

| Scripture Reference | Common Misreading | Actual First-Century Jewish Context | Key Hebrew/Greek Term |
|---|---|---|---|
| Genesis 1:1-3 | Daoist: tohu vavohu = wuji/taiji primordial chaos; creation as emanation | Tohu vavohu describes an unformed state awaiting God's ordering speech, not philosophical void. Creation is by a personal God's command, not impersonal emanation. True dialogue partner is ANE myth (Enuma Elish), not Daodejing. | **tohu vavohu** (תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ) = formless/empty; not a substance to be stirred |
| Genesis 1:2 | Daoist: ruach Elohim = Daoist qi (cosmic energy); Shinto: parallel to Izanagi stirring chaos sea | Ruach Elohim is YHWH's personal, volitional action (merachefet = hovering/brooding like a mother eagle, Deut 32:11), not impersonal flowing energy. God breathes with will and purpose. | **ruach** (רוּחַ) = spirit/breath/wind of God (personal); **merachefet** (מְרַחֶפֶת) = hovering/brooding |
| Genesis 1:26-28 | Dominionism/Seven Mountain Mandate: "dominion" over earth = Christians must rule all social institutions | Radah (rule) and kavash (subdue) apply to animals and land only, never human institutions. Gen 2:15 defines it as avad (serve) and shamar (guard) -- stewardship, not conquest. | **radah** (רָדָה) = manage animals; **kavash** (כָּבַשׁ) = subdue land; **avad** (עָבַד) = serve |
| Genesis 2:7 | Daoist: nishmat chayyim = qi (life energy); Platonic: immortal soul enters body | God personally blows (va-yippach) breath into man. Man becomes a nefesh chayyah (living being) -- not "has a soul" but "is a living being." Same term used for animals (Gen 1:20-24). No separable immortal soul. | **nefesh** (נֶפֶשׁ) = whole living being (not separable soul); **nishmat chayyim** (נִשְׁמַת חַיִּים) = breath of life |
| Genesis 4:7 | Augustinian original sin: humans totally unable to choose good | YHWH tells Cain "you can master it" (ve-attah timshol bo) -- directly affirming human moral agency against total depravity. | **timshol** (תִּמְשׇׁל) = you can rule/master it |
| Genesis 15:6 | Guan Gong parallel: Abraham's tsedaqah = heroic human yi qi (loyalty/righteousness) | Abraham did no heroic deed. He trusted YHWH's promise while childless and old. YHWH sovereignly "reckoned" (va-yachshveha) this trust as tsedaqah -- right standing in covenant relationship. The subject is God, not Abraham. | **tsedaqah** (צְדָקָה) = right standing in covenant; **he'emin** (הֶאֱמִן) = relational trust (not intellectual belief) |
| Exodus 3:7-10 | Liberation theology/CCP: Exodus = class revolution; Moses = revolutionary leader | The actor is YHWH ("I saw, I came down, I rescue"), not the people. Moses resists his commission. Purpose is not classless society but covenant worship: "Let my people go that they may serve ME" (avad). Framework is covenant (berit), not class struggle. | **ga'al** (גָּאַל) = kinship redemption; **avad** (עָבַד) = serve (transferred from Pharaoh to YHWH) |
| Exodus 20:12 | Confucian equivalence: kabbed = xiao (filial piety) including ancestor worship | Kabbed means "give weight/honor" -- a covenant obligation, not a social ethic. Its authority derives from YHWH's covenant, not family hierarchy. Torah strictly prohibits ancestor worship (Deut 18:10-12), which is central to Confucian xiao. | **kabbed** (כַּבֵּד) = make weighty/honor (covenant term); contrasts with xiao which includes ancestor veneration |
| Leviticus 19:18 | Confucian equivalence: ahavah = ren (benevolence); universal moral principle | Ahavah in Torah is a covenant-action verb (treaty loyalty, cf. Amarna Letters), not sentiment. Grounded in "I am YHWH" (ani YHWH) -- divine command, not innate human goodness. Includes obligation to love the ger (foreigner, Lev 19:34). | **ahavah** (אַהֲבָה) = covenant loyalty action; **ani YHWH** (אֲנִי יְהוָה) = "I am YHWH" (authority seal) |
| Deuteronomy 18:15-18 | Islamic claim: "from their brothers" (miqerev acheihem) = Ishmaelites, predicting Muhammad | In all of Deuteronomy, "brothers" (achim) consistently means fellow Israelites. Deut 17:15 uses identical grammar and explicitly contrasts "brothers" with "foreigner" (ish nokhri). 4QTestimonia (1st c. BCE) applies it to an Israelite prophet. | **miqerev acheihem** (מִקֶּרֶב אֲחֵיהֶם) = from among their brothers (= fellow Israelites); cf. Deut 17:15 |
| Isaiah 2:2 | Seven Mountain Mandate: "mountains" = 7 social spheres Christians must conquer | One mountain (har beit-YHWH = Temple mount), not seven. Direction is reversed: nations flow TO Zion (v'naharu elav), not believers going out to conquer. Actor is God, not humans. Parallel in Micah 4:4 ends with peaceful agriculture, not social dominance. | **har beit-YHWH** (הַר בֵּית יְהוָה) = YHWH's Temple mount (singular, specific) |
| Isaiah 7:14 | Church tradition: almah = virgin; messianic prophecy of virgin birth | Almah means "young woman of marriageable age," not virgin. Hebrew has betulah for virgin. The definite article ha-almah ("that young woman") points to someone present and known. Context is the Syro-Ephraimite Crisis (~735 BCE) -- a political sign for King Ahaz, not a prediction 700 years later. | **almah** (עַלְמָה) = young woman; **betulah** (בְּתוּלָה) = virgin (the word Isaiah did NOT use) |
| Isaiah 42:21 | Church tradition: Jesus "fulfilled" Torah so it is now obsolete | Yagdil Torah = "make Torah great/magnify." The messianic task is to enlarge and honor Torah, not complete-and-terminate it. | **yagdil Torah** (יַגְדִּיל תּוֹרָה) = magnify/make great the Torah |
| Isaiah 43:2 | Devotional (Streams in the Desert): personal promise of safety in trials | Addressed to national Israel (Ya'akov, Yisra'el -- v.1) during Babylonian exile. "Water" and "fire" refer to geographic obstacles of return and imperial oppression. Verses 3-6 describe geopolitical exchange (Egypt as ransom) and four-directional gathering of exiles. | **kofrekha** (כׇפְרְךָ) = your ransom (geopolitical); addressee is plural/national, not individual |
| Isaiah 52:13-53:12 | Modern Rabbinic Judaism: suffering servant = nation of Israel (collective reading) | Collective reading first appears with Rashi (11th c. CE). Targum Jonathan explicitly labels the servant "my servant Messiah" (avdi meshicha). Talmud Sanhedrin 98b connects Isa 53:4 to a suffering Messiah. Isaiah 49:5-6 distinguishes servant FROM Israel (servant's task: restore Israel). | **avdi meshicha** (עַבְדִּי מְשִׁיחָא) = my servant Messiah (Targum Jonathan on Isa 52:13) |
| Jeremiah 29:11 | Devotional: God's personal career plan for every individual Christian | Written to Babylonian exiles (plural aleikhem, "to you-all"). Machashavot = plans for the entire exiled nation. Shalom = national restoration (return to land, rebuild temple). Time-bound: "when 70 years for Babylon are fulfilled" (v.10). V.14: "I will gather you from all nations." | **aleikhem** (עֲלֵיכֶ֖ם) = to you (plural/national); **acharit ve-tikvah** (אַחֲרִית וְתִקְוָה) = future-and-hope (of national restoration) |
| Jeremiah 31:31-34 | Supersessionism: "new covenant" replaces and abolishes old Torah | Chadashah = renewed/refreshed (like chodesh/new moon = same moon renewed). Content of new covenant: "I will put MY Torah (torati) in their hearts" (v.33) -- same Torah, internalized. Jer 31:35-37 guarantees Israel's permanence with cosmic-level certainty. | **chadashah** (חֲדָשָׁה) = renewed (not brand-new replacement); **torati** (תּוֹרָתִי) = MY Torah (God's own, written on hearts) |
| Daniel 8:14 | Adventist: 2300 days = 2300 years pointing to 1844 CE heavenly investigative judgment | "Evening-morning" (erev boqer) echoes the daily tamid sacrifice cycle. V'nitzdak (from tsadak) means "restored to rightness," not "cleansed" (taher). Context is Antiochus IV's desecration of the earthly temple (~167 BCE). No 1st-c. source reads this as 2300 years. | **v'nitzdak** (וְנִצְדַּק) = be restored to rightness (not cleansed/kippur); **erev boqer** (עֶרֶב בֹּקֶר) = evening-morning (sacrifice cycles) |
| Psalm 46:10 | Devotional: "Be still" = quiet meditation/contemplation practice | Harpu is a military command meaning "stop fighting/drop your weapons" (cf. 2 Sam 24:16, 1 Sam 15:16). Full context is YHWH stopping wars (v.9: breaks bows, cuts spears, burns chariots). God commands warring nations to cease, not individuals to meditate. | **harpu** (הַרְפּוּ) = stop! cease! (military imperative, not contemplative) |
| Psalm 82:6 | New Age/Mormon: "you are gods" = humans possess innate divinity or can become gods | Elohim here = human judges given authority (cf. Exod 21:6, 22:8-9). V.7 immediately revokes: "you will die like mortals" (ke-adam te-mutun). It is condemnation of corrupt judges, not affirmation of human divinity. Jesus uses it as kal va-chomer argument in John 10:34-36. | **elohim** (אֱלֹהִים) = judges/authorized representatives (functional title, not ontological divinity) |
| Proverbs 10:22 | Guan Gong worship parallel: God blesses wealth like a patron deity blessing business | Birkat YHWH operates within covenantal framework (Deut 28), not transactional worship. Vv.22-25 contrast righteous vs. wicked -- wisdom literature context. Etzev ("toil/sorrow," echoing Gen 3:16-17) distinguishes YHWH's blessing from self-earned wealth. | **birkat YHWH** (בִּרְכַּת יְהוָה) = covenant blessing (not transactional patron deity response) |
| Proverbs 22:29 | Marketplace theology: God wants Christians to become elite leaders in every field | Observation sentence ("have you seen...?"), not command. Mahir = skilled craftsman. Yityatzav = "stand before" = serve kings (servant posture, not ruler posture). Context (v.28) is about respecting boundaries, opposite of expansionist ambition. | **mahir** (מָהִיר) = skilled/expert (craftsman); **yityatzav** (יִתְיַצָּב) = stand/serve before (not "become ruler") |
| Proverbs 31:10-31 | Folk religion: eshet chayil = Mazu (sea goddess) or deified female figure | Acrostic poem describing an ideal human woman. Climax (v.30): "a woman who fears YHWH (yir'at-YHWH) shall be praised" -- she is the worshiper, not the worshiped. No deification; strictly human. | **eshet chayil** (אֵשֶׁת חַיִל) = capable/valiant woman (human); **yir'at-YHWH** (יִרְאַת יְהוָה) = she fears YHWH (she worships, not is worshiped) |
| Micah 6:8 | Guan Gong parallel: mishpat = Chinese yi (righteousness/loyalty) | Mishpat = covenantal social justice (structural, institutional). Hesed = covenant loyalty (not general kindness). "Walk humbly with YOUR GOD" (im elohekha) -- the entire frame is YHWH's covenant, not standalone moral virtue. Preceded by vv.3-5 recounting Exodus. | **mishpat** (מִשְׁפָּט) = covenant justice; **hesed** (חֶסֶד) = covenant loyalty; **im elohekha** (עִם אֱלֹהֶיךָ) = with your God |
| Malachi 3:10 | Prosperity gospel: tithing guarantees personal financial blessing | Addressed to the entire post-exilic nation (ha-goy kullo, v.9). Blessing (berakhah) is agricultural: rain, no locusts, grapes don't fall (v.11). Covenant conditional language (Deut 28 framework), not personal investment principle. Jesus taught the opposite (Luke 6:20, Mark 10:25). | **berakhah** (בְּרָכָה) = covenant blessing (agricultural, national); **ma'aser** (מַעֲשֵׂר) = tithe (temple support system, not personal investment) |
| Matthew 5:17-20 | Church tradition: Jesus "fulfilled" (completed) the law, so Christians are free from it | Plerosai corresponds to Hebrew qiyyem = "establish, correctly interpret, make stand." Opposite of katalysai/batel = "abolish by wrong interpretation." V.18: not one yod or tag passes until heaven and earth pass. V.19: whoever breaks smallest commandment is least. V.20: righteousness must exceed Pharisees'. | **plerosai** (πληρῶσαι) = Hebrew **qiyyem** (קיים) = establish/uphold Torah; NOT "complete-and-terminate" |
| John 1:1 | Daoist: logos = Dao (impersonal cosmic principle); JW: "a god" (polytheistic reading) | Logos corresponds to Hebrew davar (YHWH's creative speech, Ps 33:6) and hokhmah (wisdom, Prov 8:22-31). Personal, volitional, relational. Theos without article in 1:1c is qualitative (Colwell's Rule), not indefinite. Writing against Shema backdrop (Deut 6:4). | **davar** (דָּבָר) = YHWH's personal creative word; NOT impersonal Dao |
| John 3:16 | Church tradition: proves Trinity, original sin, penal substitutionary atonement | Bronze serpent analogy (v.14, Num 21:8-9): "look and live" logic, not substitution. "God loved" = hesed (covenant loyalty) extended to world. "Only" (monogenes) = unique (yachid), not "begotten." Yetzer (Gen 4:7, 6:5) not original sin. Kafar = cleansing/covering, not penal substitution. Vv.20-21 emphasize deeds (erga). | **monogenes** (μονογενής) = unique/one-of-a-kind (not "begotten"); **kafar** (כָּפַר) = cover/cleanse (not penal substitution) |
| John 8:32 | CCP/Three-Self: "truth" = Marxism; freedom = political liberation | Aletheia corresponds to Hebrew emet = reliable, faithful, YHWH's character (not ideology). Context (8:31): truth = "my teaching." Context (8:34): freedom = from sin's slavery (doulos tes hamartias). Mishnah Avot 6:2 parallel: true freedom comes through Torah study. | **emet** (אֱמֶת) = faithfulness/reliability (YHWH's character); **aletheia** (ἀλήθεια) in context = Jesus's teaching, not political theory |
| John 10:30 | Hindu: "I and the Father are one" = Atman-is-Brahman (monistic identity) | Hen (ἕν) is neuter "one" (unity of purpose), not masculine heis (same person/being). Jesus himself explains via Ps 82:6 using kal va-chomer argument (John 10:34-36), lowering claim to Torah-acceptable range. Shaliach principle: agent represents sender. Shema excludes monism. | **hen** (ἕν) = neuter one (unity of purpose/action); NOT **heis** (εἷς) = same being |
| John 14:6 | Hindu/Yiguandao: "I am the way" = all religions lead to same truth (Ekam Sat) | Second half explicitly excludes pluralism: "no one comes to the Father except through me." Derekh/emet/chayyim are Torah concepts (Torah = way, faithfulness, life source). Exclusive agency claim (shaliach), not multireligious validation. | **derekh** (דֶּרֶךְ) = Torah's path; **emet** (אֱמֶת) = YHWH's faithfulness; exclusive shaliach claim |
| John 14:12 | New Age: "greater works" = supernatural powers achievable through meditation/energy healing | Meizona = greater in scope (geography, time, numbers), not supernatural upgrade. Condition: "in my name" (en to onomati mou) = under sender's authority (shaliach principle). Cause: "because I go to the Father" (mission expansion). No 1st-c. meditation/energy framework. | **shaliach** (שָׁלִיחַ) = authorized agent; greater works = expanded mission scope |
| John 14:16-17 | Islamic: parakletos was originally periklutos (= Ahmad/Muhammad) | Zero manuscripts (of 5,800+) read periklutos. Different roots entirely (para+kaleo vs. peri+klyo). V.17: pneuma (spirit), invisible, dwells IN believers. V.26: directly defined as Holy Spirit (to pneuma to hagion). Jewish peraklit = courtroom advocate. | **parakletos** (παράκλητος) = advocate/helper (legal term); v.26 equates it with **Holy Spirit** (τὸ πνεῦμα τὸ ἅγιον) |
| Acts 2:4 | Charismatic/True Jesus Church: tongues = only evidence of Spirit baptism | Pentecost "tongues" were known human languages (dialektos, v.8), not ecstatic speech. Corresponds to Shavuot/Sinai tradition (God's voice in 70 languages). Acts itself inconsistent: no tongues in 8:14-17. Paul explicitly: "Do all speak in tongues?" (1 Cor 12:30) -- mu expects "no." | **dialektos** (διάλεκτος) = known regional language (not ecstatic utterance); Paul's **mu** (μή) in 1 Cor 12:30 expects "no" |
| Acts 2:44-45 | CCP/Three-Self: early church = communist model | Peter in 5:4 explicitly affirms private property: "Before selling, wasn't it yours? After selling, wasn't the money at your disposal?" Torah protects private property (Exod 20:15,17 -- lo tignov, lo tachmod). Koinonia = voluntary sharing in covenant relationship, not state-enforced collectivism. | **koinonia** (κοινωνία) = voluntary covenant sharing; Peter affirms **private property** (Acts 5:4) |
| Romans 3:23 | Church tradition: proves Augustinian original sin doctrine | Paul writes as a 1st-c. Pharisee. Jewish concept is yetzer (inclination) -- both good (yetzer ha-tov) and evil (yetzer ha-ra), not inherited total depravity. Gen 4:7: "you CAN master it." Ezek 18:20: son does not bear father's sin. Augustine (4th-5th c.) systematized original sin. | **yetzer** (יֵצֶר) = inclination (not inherited guilt); **hēmarton** (ἥμαρτον) = sinned (aorist); context is covenant argument, not original sin proof |
| Romans 3:28 | Luther's sola fide: faith vs. works opposition; law abolished by grace | Erga nomou = boundary markers (circumcision, dietary laws, Sabbath) -- proven by 4QMMT (Dead Sea Scrolls, only other ancient use of "works of the law"). Pistis = emunah = covenant faithfulness (not mental assent). Rom 3:31: "Do we abolish law? Absolutely not! We ESTABLISH it." Rom 2:13: "doers of the law will be justified." | **erga nomou** (ἔργα νόμου) = identity boundary markers (cf. **ma'ase ha-Torah** in 4QMMT); **pistis** (πίστις) = **emunah** (אֱמוּנָה) = covenant faithfulness |
| Romans 9:11-13 | Calvinism (TULIP): individual predestination to salvation or damnation | Shenei goyim (Gen 25:23) = "two nations," not two individuals. Sane'ti (Mal 1:3) = "not chosen for covenant role" (cf. Gen 29:31 -- Leah "unloved" but not hated). Rom 9:30-32: Israel stumbled by their own choice. Rom 11:26: "all Israel will be saved." Rom 11:32: "mercy on ALL." | **goyim** (גוֹיִם) = nations (not individuals); **bachar** (בָּחַר) = choose for mission/role (not eternal destiny); **sane** (שָׂנֵא) = not chosen (covenantal, not emotional hatred) |
| Romans 11:17-24 | Supersessionism: church replaces Israel in God's plan | Olive tree = Israel. Gentiles are wild branches grafted INTO Israel's tree. "The root supports YOU, not you the root" (v.18). Broken branches can be re-grafted (v.23). V.26: "all Israel will be saved." V.29: God's gifts and calling are irrevocable (ametameleta). | **ametameleta** (ἀμεταμέλητα) = irrevocable (God's calling of Israel); Gentiles grafted INTO Israel, not replacing it |
| 1 Corinthians 15:29 | Mormon: baptism for the dead (proxy ordinance) | Paul uses 3rd person "they" (not "we") -- distancing himself. Reductio ad absurdum argument for resurrection's reality, not endorsement. No NT passage teaches/commands proxy baptism. Tevilah/mikveh in Judaism = personal repentance/purification. | **hyper** (ὑπέρ) = "because of/on account of" (not necessarily "in place of"); Paul's 3rd-person distancing |
| Galatians 6:7 | Folk religion: "sow and reap" = karma (automatic cosmic law) | Opens with "God is not mocked" (theos) -- personal covenant God governs outcomes, not impersonal law. Job 42:7 rebukes Eliphaz's mechanical retribution theology. YHWH can forgive (hesed); karma has no forgiveness mechanism. Framework: covenant, not cosmic causation. | Context: **theos** (θεός) = personal God judges (not automatic karma); YHWH's **hesed** (חֶסֶד) includes forgiveness, which karma lacks |
| Colossians 1:27 | New Age: "Christ in you" = divine spark within everyone; chakra/Reiki energy | En hymin more likely "among you" (parallel to en tois ethnesin in same verse). Mystery (mysterion/raz) = historical revelation that Messiah is now among Gentile communities. Kavod (glory) = YHWH's covenant presence, not manipulable energy. Colossians 2:8 warns against exactly this kind of philosophizing. | **en hymin** (ἐν ὑμῖν) = among you (collective, not inner spark); **kavod** (כָּבוֹד) = YHWH's weighty presence (not energy) |
| Hebrews 8:13 | Supersessionism: old covenant obsolete, Torah abolished | Kainos (not neos) = renewed/upgraded, not brand-new replacement. Heb 8:10 quotes Jer 31:33: "I will put my LAWS (nomous) in their minds" -- new covenant CONTAINS Torah. "Growing old" likely refers to temple sacrificial system (about to end in 70 CE), not Torah itself. | **kainos** (καινός) = renewed in quality (not **neos**/νέος = brand-new replacement) |
| 3 John 2 | Prosperity gospel: God wills every believer to be wealthy and healthy | Standard 1st-c. epistolary greeting formula (like "I hope this finds you well"). Euodousthai = "go well on the road," not financial prosperity. Written to one person (Gaius), not universal theological principle. Comparable greetings found in Oxyrhynchus papyri (secular letters). | **euodousthai** (εὐοδοῦσθαι) = fare well/go smoothly (epistolary convention, not prosperity doctrine) |
| Mark 16:17-18 | Charismatic: snake-handling and poison-drinking as normative Christian practice | Mark 16:9-20 (Longer Ending) absent from earliest manuscripts (Sinaiticus, Vaticanus). Eusebius and Jerome confirm "accurate copies" end at 16:8. Even if accepted, promissory language (like Ps 91:13), not prescriptive command. Halakhah vs. aggadah distinction. | Entire passage is **textually disputed** -- absent from Codex Sinaiticus (~330-360 CE) and Codex Vaticanus (~325-350 CE) |
| Luke 1:28 | Catholic: kecharitomene proves Immaculate Conception, basis for Marian devotion | Kecharitomene = "having been graced" (passive -- she receives grace, not its source). Same root charitoo in Eph 1:6 applies to ALL believers. Mary calls God "my Savior" (Luke 1:47). Vulgate's gratia plena ("full of grace") shifts to active sense. Original sin concept itself is 4th-5th c. (Augustine). | **kecharitomene** (κεχαριτωμένη) = passively graced (not inherently graceful); Vulgate **gratia plena** is misleading active rendering |

## Notes

- Passages are sorted by canonical order (Hebrew Bible first, then New Testament in traditional sequence).
- "Key term" column identifies the Hebrew or Greek word whose correct understanding resolves the misreading.
- Multiple response files sometimes identify the same passage; the entry above synthesizes all occurrences.
- Sources referenced across responses: Sefaria.org (Hebrew), Bible Gateway RCUV, Dead Sea Scrolls, Targum Jonathan, Babylonian Talmud, Josephus.
