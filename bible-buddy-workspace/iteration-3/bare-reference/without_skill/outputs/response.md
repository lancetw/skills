**Romans 3:23**

> "for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God,"

This is one of the most frequently cited verses in the New Testament. The apostle Paul wrote this in his letter to the Romans as part of a larger argument about the universal need for salvation. In context (Romans 3:21-26), Paul is establishing that no one -- whether Jew or Gentile -- can achieve righteousness through their own efforts or by following the Law. Everyone has sinned and therefore falls short of God's standard of glory and holiness.

This verse serves as a theological foundation for the Christian doctrine of grace: because all humans share this condition, salvation must come through faith in Jesus Christ rather than through human merit (as Paul continues in verse 24: "and all are justified freely by his grace through the redemption that came by Christ Jesus").

The Chinese Union Version (CUV) renders it as:

> 因為世人都犯了罪，虧缺了神的榮耀。
