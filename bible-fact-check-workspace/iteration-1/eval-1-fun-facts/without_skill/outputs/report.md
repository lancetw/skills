## 1. Factual Errors

### Line 8 — Luther and Romans 3:28
> 路德在翻譯羅馬書 3:28 時加了 allein（唯獨），但希臘原文沒有這個字，而雅各書 2:24 正好否定了它

**Issue**: The claim that James 2:24 "negates" (否定) Luther's addition is an oversimplification that takes a theological side. James 2:24 says justification is "not by faith alone," but the relationship between Paul and James is a complex exegetical question. Saying James "negates" Luther's reading presents one interpretation as settled fact. Consider softening to "呼應了不同的觀點" or simply noting the tension.

### Line 13 — "Two hundred years of development"
> 三位一體教義經歷了兩百年的發展

**Issue**: From Theophilus (~180 CE) to Constantinople (381 CE) is about 200 years, but Trinitarian thinking traces back further (e.g., Matthew 28:19, 2 Cor 13:14). The phrasing implies the doctrine was invented over 200 years, which is misleading. The *terminology and creedal formulation* took ~200 years; the underlying theological convictions are arguably earlier.

### Line 14 — Altar call dating
> 呼召禱告（altar call）是 1820 年代 Charles Finney 發明的

**Issue**: Finney popularized the "anxious bench" in the 1830s (not 1820s). His *Lectures on Revivals of Religion* was published in 1835. The 1820s date is slightly early. Also, earlier Methodist camp meetings (early 1800s) had similar practices; Finney refined rather than invented the concept.

### Line 19 — TULIP and Dort
> 加爾文主義的 TULIP 五要點是 1618-1619 年多特會議制定的

**Issue**: The Synod of Dort produced the Canons of Dort, but the TULIP acronym itself is a much later English mnemonic (likely early 20th century). The entry conflates the Canons with the TULIP acronym. More precisely: the *five doctrinal points* were formulated at Dort; "TULIP" as a label came centuries later.

### Line 45 — Pilate Inscription
> 彼拉多銘文 1961 年在凱撒利亞出土，上面寫著「猶太長官」(praefectus Iudaeae)

**Issue**: The readable text on the stone is partially damaged. The reconstruction "praefectus Iudaeae" involves some restoration of broken text (`[PRAEF]ECTUS IUDA[EA]E`). The entry presents the reading as fully legible when it is partially reconstructed. Minor but worth noting for precision.

### Line 51 — Notovitch refutation timeline
> Max Muller 同年透過書信取得否認

**Issue**: Max Mueller published his critique in *The Nineteenth Century* in 1894, but his method was not merely "obtaining a denial through correspondence" -- he marshaled philological and historical arguments. The characterization is somewhat reductive.

### Line 53 — Tohu vavohu and Daoism
> 但「混沌」帶有道教莊子的寓意

**Issue**: The claim that 混沌 "carries Daoist Zhuangzi connotations" is debatable. 混沌 is a common Chinese word used in many contexts beyond Daoism. While Zhuangzi's "hundun" parable is well known, the word itself is not exclusively Daoist. The critique has some merit but overstates the Daoist association.

### Line 56 — Daniel 8:14 and Adventism
> 這個誤譯催生了安息日會 1844 年「查案審判」教義

**Issue**: The Adventist "Investigative Judgment" doctrine was primarily based on the KJV translation "then shall the sanctuary be cleansed" (following the LXX katharisthesetai), not the CUV (和合本). The CUV was published in 1919, well after 1844. The entry implies the CUV translation caused the Adventist doctrine, but the causal chain is reversed -- the KJV/LXX tradition preceded both. The point about the Hebrew root tsadak vs. taher is linguistically correct.

### Line 85 — Jesus's daily language
> 耶穌的日常語言是亞蘭文，不是希伯來文

**Issue**: This is an oversimplification of a debated scholarly question. While Aramaic was the lingua franca of first-century Galilee, many scholars (e.g., Shmuel Safrai, David Bivin) argue that Hebrew was also widely spoken. The Dead Sea Scrolls and Bar Kokhba letters demonstrate Hebrew was a living language. Stating definitively that Jesus's daily language was "Aramaic, not Hebrew" goes beyond what scholarship can firmly establish.

### Line 98 — Paul wrote the most NT books
> 卻寫了新約最多的書卷

**Issue**: This depends on how you count. Paul is traditionally attributed 13 epistles, but authorship of several is disputed. If we count by volume of text, Luke-Acts (attributed to Luke) comprises a larger portion of the NT than all Pauline letters combined. The entry should specify "most books/letters" vs. "most text."

### Line 112 — Deuteronomy 28 verse counts
> 申命記 28 章的祝福（28:1-14）只有 14 節，咒詛（28:15-68）卻有 54 節，將近四倍長

**Issue**: 54/14 = 3.86x. "將近四倍" is a reasonable approximation by verse count. However, "四倍長" refers to length, not just verse count. In terms of actual Hebrew text length, the ratio may differ since verse lengths vary. Minor issue.

## 2. Verse Reference Errors

### Line 93 — Luke 10:1 — Seventy or Seventy-Two?
> 路 10:1 差遣 70 人

**Issue**: The manuscript tradition is divided between 70 and 72. Many modern critical texts (NA28/UBS5) prefer 72 (hebdomekonta duo). The entry states 70 without noting the textual variant.

### Line 78 — 1 Chronicles 16:22
> 「歷代志上 16:22 不可難為我受膏的人」

**Issue**: This verse also appears as Psalm 105:15. Both references are correct, but the Psalm reference is more commonly cited in popular usage. Not an error, but noting the parallel could strengthen the entry.

## 4. Oversimplification

### Line 7 — pistis = emunah
> 「因信稱義」的「信」(pistis) 對應希伯來文 emunah，意思是「忠信/信實」，不是「相信一套教義」

**Issue**: pistis has a semantic range that includes trust, faithfulness, AND belief/conviction. Reducing it to only "loyalty/faithfulness" overcorrects in the opposite direction. The entry rightly pushes back against "believing a set of doctrines" but oversimplifies by implying pistis/emunah excludes cognitive assent entirely.

### Line 36 — chata' = "miss the mark"
> 希伯來文 chata'（罪）的原意是「射偏目標」

**Issue**: While the etymological connection to "missing a mark" (Judges 20:16) is genuine, the "original meaning" claim is an etymological fallacy. In actual usage throughout the Hebrew Bible, chata' covers deliberate sin, rebellion, and moral failure -- not just "missing." The entry's contrast with "Western legal framework" is also somewhat of a straw man.

### Line 60 — Original sin
> 「原罪」本身是奧古斯丁 4-5 世紀的概念，第一世紀猶太人用 yetzer（傾向）框架，根本沒有「遺傳的罪」

**Issue**: While Augustine systematized the doctrine, earlier Church Fathers (Irenaeus, Tertullian, Cyprian) had related concepts. Also, some Second Temple Jewish texts (e.g., 4 Ezra 3:21-22, 7:118) do discuss Adam's sin affecting descendants, so "根本沒有" (absolutely none) overstates the case.

### Line 75 — 1 Cor 14:34-35 as marginal gloss
> 是後人旁注的特徵

**Issue**: The interpolation theory is one scholarly position (Philip Payne, Gordon Fee), but it is not the consensus. Many scholars accept the verses as original Pauline text with various explanations for the textual displacement. Presenting the interpolation theory as fact is one-sided.

### Line 95 — Jesus as tekton
> 耶穌可能不是木匠——可 6:3 的 tekton 是「工匠/建造者」

**Issue**: The entry is fair in saying "可能不是" (possibly not), but the argument about Galilean stone is speculative. A tekton could work with wood, stone, or both. The Sepphoris connection lacks direct evidence linking Jesus to that site.

## 9. Additional Quality Issues

### Line 9 — Welch motivation
> 葡萄汁聖餐是 1869 年美國禁酒運動的產物（Thomas Welch 用巴斯德殺菌法）

**Issue**: Welch was motivated by his Methodist beliefs about communion wine specifically; the broader temperance movement connection came later through his son Charles Welch's marketing. Calling it a "product of the temperance movement" is slightly imprecise.

### Line 21 — Golden Rule attribution
> 馬太福音 7:12 的黃金律則來自利未記 19:18 和拉比希列的教導

**Issue**: Saying the Golden Rule "comes from" (來自) Leviticus and Hillel implies direct derivation. The relationship is more about shared ethical tradition and echoes. Consider "呼應" (echoes) rather than "來自" (comes from).

### Line 141 — Dead Sea Scrolls Isaiah
> 死海古卷中的以賽亞書卷軸（1QIsaᵃ）是目前最完整的希伯來聖經手抄本

**Issue**: 1QIsaᵃ contains only the book of Isaiah (all 66 chapters), not the entire Hebrew Bible. "最完整的希伯來聖經手抄本" could be misread as the most complete manuscript of the entire Hebrew Bible. Clarify that it is the most complete single biblical book manuscript from the DSS.
