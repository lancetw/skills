## 2. Factual Errors and Questionable Claims

### 2a. Genesis 2:7 — Luzhenzhong translation claim

> "Luzhenzhong's 有生命的活人 are more accurate"

**Issue**: The entry claims CCV uses 有生命的人. This should be verified. CCV (當代譯本/新漢語譯本) renderings vary across editions and the specific wording attributed here may not match the published text exactly.

### 2b. Isaiah 7:14 — Luzhenzhong renders 少婦

> "Luzhenzhong's 少婦 is the most faithful Chinese translation"

**Issue**: 少婦 literally means "young married woman," which goes beyond the semantic range of עַלְמָה. The Hebrew almah means "young woman (of marriageable age)" — it does not specify marital status. Calling 少婦 "the most faithful" is an overclaim. A truly neutral translation would simply be 年輕女子 or 少女. Luzhenzhong's choice imports a "married" assumption that is not in the Hebrew either.

### 2c. Isaiah 7:14 — betulah claim

> "Hebrew has a specific word for virgin: בְּתוּלָה (betulah), which Isaiah did NOT use"

**Issue — oversimplification**: While this is the standard critical argument, it oversimplifies. betulah does not always mean "virgin" in the strict biological sense either — Joel 1:8 uses betulah for a woman mourning the husband of her youth (בַּעַל נְעוּרֶיהָ), implying she was married. The entry presents betulah = virgin as settled fact when the semantics of both almah and betulah are debated in scholarship. The core point (almah ≠ virgin) is defensible, but the supporting argument needs nuance.

### 2d. Daniel 8:14 — Adventist doctrine claim

> "This mistranslation enabled the Adventist 'Investigative Judgment' doctrine."

**Issue**: While historically connected, the Adventist Investigative Judgment doctrine was primarily enabled by the KJV English rendering "shall be cleansed" (following the Vulgate), not by Chinese translations. The Chinese translation followed the same tradition, but saying the Chinese "enabled" the doctrine is misleading — the doctrine was formulated in English by William Miller and later Adventist theologians. The Chinese followed, not led.

### 2e. Romans 3:28 — 4QMMT reference

> "ἔργα νόμου ... cf. 4QMMT: מעשי התורה = specific Jewish ethnic boundary markers (circumcision, dietary laws, Sabbath), NOT 'all good deeds'"

**Issue — oversimplification**: The New Perspective on Paul (Dunn, Wright) interpretation is presented as established fact. This is one scholarly position, but it is contested. The 4QMMT parallel is suggestive but not conclusive — 4QMMT's מעשי התורה refers to specific halakhic rulings, not necessarily "ethnic boundary markers" in Dunn's sense. Many Pauline scholars (Westerholm, Schreiner, Das) argue erga nomou is broader than just boundary markers. The entry should acknowledge this is a debated reading, not present it as "what the original actually means."

### 2f. Romans 3:28 — "All Chinese versions follow Luther"

> "All Chinese versions follow Luther"

**Issue**: This is an overstatement. Chinese versions translate the Greek text (which does say χωρὶς ἔργων νόμου), not Luther's German. Luther famously added "allein" (alone) to Romans 3:28 — Chinese versions do NOT add 單單/唯獨 in the main text. The Chinese translations are following the Greek here, not Luther. The theological framework may be influenced by Protestant tradition, but the claim "follow Luther" is misleading.

### 2g. Galatians 6:16 — "Paul's consistent usage"

> "In Paul's consistent usage across all his letters, Ἰσραήλ always refers to ethnic Israel"

**Issue**: This is the position of scholars like Schreiner, but it is debated. Romans 9:6 ("not all who are descended from Israel are Israel") is often cited as a counterexample where Paul redefines Israel. The claim of absolute consistency is stronger than the evidence warrants.

### 2h. 1 Corinthians 15:29 — LDS doctrine claim

> "RCUV/Luzhenzhong's proxy-baptism reading enabled LDS (Mormon) doctrine of baptism for the dead"

**Issue**: The LDS doctrine of baptism for the dead was based on English translations (KJV: "baptized for the dead"), not Chinese translations. Chinese translations followed the same rendering tradition but did not "enable" the LDS doctrine. The LDS church was founded in English-speaking America in 1830.

## 3. Verse Reference Issues

### 3a. Genesis 2:7 — nefesh chayyah for animals

> "Same term used for animals in Gen 1:20-21,24"

**Issue**: Gen 1:20 uses נֶפֶשׁ חַיָּה, correct. Gen 1:21 uses the plural construct (different form). Gen 1:24 uses נֶפֶשׁ חַיָּה, correct. The reference is essentially accurate but including 1:21 is slightly imprecise since the exact form differs (though the concept is the same).

### 3b. Systemic 氣 row — Gen 1:2

> "Gen 1:2" listed under 氣 for רוּחַ/נִשְׁמָה

**Issue**: Gen 1:2 has רוּחַ אֱלֹהִים (ruach elohim), which RCUV translates as 上帝的靈 (God's spirit), not 氣. The 氣 issue in Gen 2:7 is about נִשְׁמָה, not רוּחַ. Listing Gen 1:2 under the 氣 pattern conflates two different Hebrew words and two different translation issues. Gen 1:2's translation issue belongs under the 靈 pattern, not 氣.

## 5. Oversimplifications

### 5a. John 1:1 — 道 criticism

The entry treats 道 as purely Daoist, but the Chinese Christian tradition has debated and intentionally adopted 道 since the Nestorian Stele (781 CE). The choice was not naive — early Chinese Christian translators (including Morrison, 1823) deliberately chose 道 as the best available term, aware of the Daoist connotations but judging it the closest Chinese equivalent for the concept of divine creative word. The entry does not acknowledge this long translation history or the arguments in favor of 道.

### 5b. Exodus 20:12 — "structurally incompatible"

> "The frameworks are structurally incompatible"

**Issue**: This is an overstatement. While there are important differences between kabbed and xiao, the claim of structural incompatibility ignores genuine overlap (both involve honoring parents, both are embedded in broader ethical systems). The specific concern about ancestor worship extending from xiao is valid, but "structurally incompatible" is too strong.

### 5c. Romans 3:22 — pistis Christou

The entry correctly identifies the objective/subjective genitive ambiguity but then says "the main text chooses the Reformation reading." The objective genitive reading ("faith in Christ") is not uniquely a "Reformation reading" — it is the majority reading across most traditions (Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant). Calling it specifically a "Reformation reading" implies it is a sectarian choice when it is in fact the traditional majority interpretation.

## 6. Anachronisms

### 6a. Romans 3:28 — projecting modern debate onto Paul

> "Paul's actual question was 'Must Gentiles become Jews to enter the covenant?' not 'Can humans earn salvation by being good?'"

**Issue**: This presents the New Perspective on Paul as if it were Paul's own self-understanding. While the New Perspective is a valuable corrective, presenting it as "what Paul actually meant" vs. what Luther thought is itself anachronistic — it replaces one interpretive framework with another and presents it as objective.

## 10. Missing Context / Other Issues

### 10a. No mention of CUV/RCUV publication dates

The file references CUV, RCUV, CNVT, CCV, and Luzhenzhong but never states their publication dates. For a reference file about translation bias, knowing when each translation was produced is important context (CUV 1919, RCUV 2010, CNVT 1992, etc.).

### 10b. Systemic pattern: 成全 for πληρῶσαι

> "In rabbinic context, πληρῶσαι = קיים (qiyyem) = 'establish/correctly interpret'"

**Issue**: The claim that πληρῶσαι = קיים in rabbinic context is a specific scholarly argument (often associated with David Bivin and scholars of Jewish background to the NT). This is not universally accepted. The equation relies on assumed Aramaic/Hebrew substrate behind Matthew's Greek, which is debated. It should be marked as one interpretation, not stated as fact.

### 10c. Table header inconsistency

The column header says "What the Hebrew/Greek Original Actually Means" — the word "Actually" implies objective certainty, but many entries acknowledge ambiguity (Romans 3:22, Hebrews 8:13, Colossians 1:27). The header tone is inconsistent with the nuanced content in several entries.
